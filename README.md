## 📚 Background

### A Brief History and Origins
Sweet and Sour Pork has its roots in Cantonese cuisine, particularly from the southern regions of China where sweet and sour flavor profiles are commonly used. Over time, this dish gained global popularity, especially after being introduced by Chinese immigrants to Western audiences in the 20th century. Today, it is one of the most recognizable and widely served Chinese dishes in the world.

![Sweet and Sour Pork](img/sweet-and-sour-pork.png)

### Why This Dish Is Celebrated
The appeal of Sweet and Sour Pork lies in its vibrant colors, contrast of textures, and the balanced flavor between the tang of vinegar and the sweetness of sugar and ketchup. Beyond taste, it also carries cultural symbolism, especially during festive periods such as Chinese New Year, where it represents happiness and good fortune.

### Fun Facts
Global Popularity: One of the most frequently ordered items in Chinese restaurants, especially across the U.S. and Canada.

Regional Variations: Some versions substitute pork with chicken, tofu, or seafood, reflecting local preferences and dietary needs.

Symbolism: The harmony of sweet and sour flavors reflects a key principle in Chinese cooking—balance and unity.

## 📦 Inputs (Ingredients)

### Main Ingredients

#### Pork Marinade:

* 500g pork tenderloin (bite-sized pieces)
* 1 tbsp soy sauce
* 1 tbsp cornstarch

#### Sweet and Sour Sauce:

* 1/4 cup ketchup
* 1/4 cup rice vinegar (or white vinegar)
* 1/4 cup sugar
* 1 tbsp soy sauce

#### For Stir-Frying:

* 1 tbsp vegetable oil
* 1 bell pepper (chunked)
* 1 onion (chunked)
* 1/2 cup pineapple (fresh or canned)
* Optional: 1 sliced carrot

## 🔧 Procedure

### Preparation

1. **Marinate Pork** (15–30 min)

   * Mix pork, soy sauce, and cornstarch in a bowl.
   * Let rest to tenderize.

2. **Chop Vegetables** (10 min)

   * Cut bell pepper, onion, and optional carrot.
   * Drain pineapple if canned.

---

### Cooking

1. **Heat Oil**

   * Medium-high heat in wok or skillet.

2. **Fry Pork** (5–7 min)

   * Cook until golden and fully done.
   * Remove with slotted spoon.

3. **Stir-Fry Veggies** (3–4 min)

   * Add pepper, onion, and carrot.
   * Stir-fry until tender-crisp.

4. **Add Sauce & Pineapple** (2 min)

   * Mix sauce ingredients in bowl and pour into wok.
   * Stir with pineapple.

5. **Combine & Finish** (2–3 min)

   * Return pork to wok.
   * Heat through and thicken sauce.

## 🔎 Validation

* Visual check: vibrant colors and glossy sauce.
* Texture: pork is tender, vegetables are crisp.
* Taste: balanced sweet and sour flavor.
* Optional: Verify internal temp for pork (\~63°C or 145°F).

---

## 🚨 Risks and Mitigations

### Risk 1: **Flavor Imbalance**

* **Mitigation**: Taste sauce before use. Adjust sugar or vinegar.

### Risk 2: **Overcooked Pork**

* **Mitigation**: Stick to cooking time; rest briefly after frying.

### Risk 3: **Unavailable Ingredients**

* **Mitigation**: Use suggested alternatives.

---

## 🔁 Fallback Plans

* **If sauce consistency is wrong**: Add water (too thick) or reduce further (too thin).
* **If overall flavor is off**: Add condiments like soy or chili sauce.
* **If dish underwhelms**: Turn into a mixed vegetable stir-fry with extra sauce and vegetables.

## 🧰 Troubleshooting

| Symptom            | Likely Cause    | Solution                |
| ------------------ | --------------- | ----------------------- |
| Pork is dry        | Overcooked      | Reduce fry time         |
| Sauce is too thick | Too much starch | Add water               |
| Veggies are soggy  | Overcooked      | Stir-fry on high heat   |
| Taste is flat      | Poor balance    | Adjust sugar or vinegar |
